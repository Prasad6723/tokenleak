# USER_INTERVIEWS

## Interview 1

**Name:** R.K.  
**Role:** Indie Hacker / Solo SaaS Founder  
**Company Stage:** Pre-revenue

### Direct Quotes
- "I honestly have no clue how much AI tooling I waste every month."
- "I subscribed to multiple tools because everyone on Twitter recommended them."
- "I would absolutely use a free audit tool before renewing annual plans."

### Most surprising thing
The founder cared less about total spend and more about overlapping subscriptions that provided similar value.

### What it changed about my design
I added clearer downgrade recommendations and focused more heavily on duplicate-tool detection.

## Interview 2

**Name:** A.S.  
**Role:** Engineering Lead  
**Company Stage:** 15-person startup

### Direct Quotes
- "Our AI costs increased faster than our cloud bill this quarter."
- "Developers request tools individually and finance barely tracks it."
- "We would probably switch vendors if savings were obvious enough."

### Most surprising thing
The company did not centrally manage AI subscriptions, leading to multiple unused seats.

### What it changed about my design
I emphasized seat-based optimization logic and team-size analysis in the audit engine.

## Interview 3

**Name:** M.P.  
**Role:** Student Founder  
**Company Stage:** Early MVP

### Direct Quotes
- "I mainly use free tiers but APIs still surprise me sometimes."
- "I care more about predictability than absolute cheapest pricing."
- "A shareable savings report would actually be cool to post."

### Most surprising thing
The founder viewed audit reports as social proof and content, not just cost optimization.

### What it changed about my design
I paid more attention to the presentation quality of the results page and sharing flow.
