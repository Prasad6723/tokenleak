# ECONOMICS

If Credex deployed this tool publicly, the economics could work extremely well because the audience already has high purchase intent. Users only search for AI spend optimization after experiencing pain from growing bills.

Assuming an average successful customer purchases discounted AI credits worth $6,000 annually through Credex, and Credex captures roughly a 15% margin, a converted customer could realistically be worth around $900 gross profit over one year.

The acquisition costs for this tool could remain very low because distribution is content-driven and community-driven rather than ad-driven.

Estimated CAC by channel:
- Product Hunt: ~$15 per qualified lead
- Twitter/X organic: ~$5–10 per lead
- Founder Slack communities: near $0
- SEO/content: ~$20–30 initially but decreasing over time

If 1,000 users complete audits:
- 25% submit email
- 8% request consultation
- 20% of consultations convert to paying customers

That becomes:
- 1,000 audits
- 250 leads
- 20 consultations
- 4 customers

At ~$900 gross profit per converted customer:
4 × $900 = $3,600 gross profit

If acquisition costs remain under ~$1,000 total for those 1,000 users, the economics become attractive very quickly.

For the tool to drive $1M ARR within 18 months, several assumptions must become true:
- Consistent viral sharing of audit results
- Strong SEO around AI pricing comparisons
- High conversion from audits to consultations
- Expansion into enterprise AI procurement workflows

Example path:
- 50,000 audits/month
- 10,000 captured leads
- 500 consultations
- 100 converted customers/month
- Average annual customer value: $10,000+

At that scale, annual recurring revenue could cross $1M if the operational side of Credex successfully fulfills the generated demand.

The biggest risk is that AI pricing changes rapidly. The audit engine and pricing data would require constant maintenance to preserve trust and accuracy.
