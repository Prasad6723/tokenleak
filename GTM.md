# GTM

The ideal target user for this product is a technical founder or engineering manager at a startup with 5–50 employees already heavily using AI tools. Specifically, companies building software products, AI wrappers, SaaS tools, or developer tooling are the best fit because they tend to accumulate overlapping subscriptions quickly.

Right before wanting this tool, users are usually searching for things like:
- "best AI coding assistant"
- "Cursor vs Copilot"
- "reduce OpenAI API costs"
- "best ChatGPT plan for teams"
- "AI tool stack for startups"

They also complain about AI spending on X/Twitter, Reddit, and founder communities after monthly billing cycles.

The target audience hangs out in:
- r/startups
- r/SideProject
- r/Entrepreneur
- Indie Hackers
- Hacker News
- AI engineering Discord servers
- Founder Twitter/X circles
- SaaS founder Slack groups

To get the first 100 users without paid ads, I would focus on distribution-first content.

First, I would post short audit screenshots publicly on X with captions like:
"We audited a 12-person startup spending $1,400/month on AI tools. They could cut it to $620."

Second, I would manually DM indie founders and engineering leads offering free audits in exchange for feedback.

Third, I would launch on Product Hunt and Hacker News with a very strong visual results page showing real savings examples.

Fourth, I would create a free "AI Spend Calculator" embed widget bloggers could place inside AI tooling comparison articles.

The unfair distribution advantage for Credex is access to real pricing inefficiencies and discounted infrastructure credits. Most competitors can suggest cheaper plans, but Credex can actually help startups reduce costs directly after the audit.

If this works in week one, traction would look like:
- 100+ completed audits
- 20% email capture rate
- 10 consultation requests
- 3–5 high-value startup leads
- strong sharing of audit screenshots on X and LinkedIn

The biggest advantage of the product is that it creates value before asking for anything from the user. That makes sharing and word-of-mouth much more natural.
